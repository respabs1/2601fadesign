What is the Olympiad Math App?

📚

What We Offer

The Olympiad Math App is a comprehensive learning platform designed specifically for mathematics olympiad preparation. We provide structured, bite-sized lessons that guide students through challenging mathematical concepts, from basic foundations to competition-level problems.

🎯

The Problem We Solve

Students struggle with olympiad mathematics because they lack quality structured guidance. Most platforms either oversimplify concepts or present problems without clear explanations. We bridge this gap by combining expert-level explanations with progressive problem-solving, just like learning from an experienced coach.

💡

Why It Matters

Olympiad mathematics develops critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and mathematical maturity. These skills are invaluable for future STEM careers and academic excellence. Yet access to quality preparation resources is limited for most students worldwide.

✨

What Makes Us Unique

Structured Learning Path: Courses follow a logical progression, building knowledge systematically. Geometry Focus (Euclidea-style): We emphasize geometric reasoning with visual explanations and construction problems. Real Olympiad Problems: Every problem comes from actual competitions. Expert Guidance: Created by olympiad medalists who understand exactly what students need to succeed.

Interview with Sponsor Sailaubay Beksultan

Real feedback from a top Olympiad mentor

Ertore: Beksultan abi, thank you for agreeing to be our potential sponsor. We are developing an app for math Olympiad preparation and want to ensure it is truly valuable for the community.
Ilyas: Given your gold and silver medals at international and national Olympiads, how has your vision of preparation changed since you were a participant yourself?
Beksultan abi: When I was competing, preparation was chaotic; we searched for old PDFs on forums and translated them manually. I now realize that Olympiad math is about intuition rather than rote memorization. My vision is that preparation should be structured and accessible, not a "survival of the fittest."
Ertore: What digital tools or platforms do you consider most effective for deep mathematical study at the moment?
Beksultan abi: Currently, the best tools are Art of Problem Solving (AoPS) and GeoGebra for visualization. However, a mobile solution that guides you through specific topics like advanced geometry is still missing.
Ilyas: How critical do you consider the lack of structured mobile trainers for studying complex topics like the Miquel point or the Simson line?
Beksultan abi: This is a critical issue. Many students quit Olympiads because they "trip" on advanced topics. Without a coach to explain a concept like the Miquel point, finding clear examples and relevant problems in one place is nearly impossible.
Ertore: Do you see this project as more of a personal benefit for individual students or as a vital tool for the country's entire Olympiad community?
Beksultan abi: I see this as a vital tool for the entire community. It can serve as a "social elevator" for talented students who lack access to top-tier schools or private tutors.
Ilyas: What specific results should our project achieve for you, as an expert, to consider it worthy of support and investment?
Beksultan abi: For me, a project is "worthy" if it is effective. If a student can solve a Republican-level problem after an hour in the app that they couldn't solve before, that is a success.
Ertore: How important is the social component to you as a sponsor—for example, making this knowledge accessible to students in rural schools?
Beksultan abi: This is my priority. I will support a project if it helps bridge the gap between elite city lyceums and rural schools; knowledge should be accessible to all.
Ilyas: Who do you think we should target first: beginners who need the basics, or advanced students preparing for the National Olympiad?
Beksultan abi: You should target advanced beginners and intermediate students. Students already at the international level might find it too simple, but those preparing for regional and national stages are the perfect fit.
Ertore: What is the main shift in a student’s skills or mindset that should occur after using our app?
Beksultan abi: The main shift should be moving from "I am afraid of this problem" to "I have a clear set of theorems and algorithms to solve this".
Ilyas: What features do you consider mandatory for an app of this type to be more useful than a standard textbook?
Beksultan abi: You must include interactive diagrams. In geometry, it is crucial to see how the figure changes when you move points. You also need "Hints" that nudge the student toward the answer without giving it away immediately.
Ertore: Are there any features (e.g., chats or excessive animation) that you believe would only distract Olympiad students from their work?
Beksultan abi: Remove the noise. Complex social mechanics or chats often distract Olympiad students from deep work; you need a clean, focused interface.
Ilyas: Considering this is a one-semester student project, what volume of content (number of topics) seems realistic to you for a high-quality launch?
Beksultan abi: Do not try to cover all of mathematics in one semester. Build 5–7 topics perfectly—for example, a module on "Advanced Triangle Geometry". Quality of content is more important than the number of sections.
Ertore: Do you have any concerns regarding the technical implementation of interactive tasks (similar to Euclidea) by a student team?
Beksultan abi: Implementing a tool like Euclidea is a challenge for students. Start with simple interactive proofs before moving to a full-scale construction engine.
Ilyas: What could cause you to lose interest in our project entirely during the development process?
Beksultan abi: I would lose interest if I saw mathematical errors. In our field, one incorrect lemma kills the credibility of the product forever.
Ertore: What is the main piece of advice you would give us before we start preparing the project for Kickstarter?
Beksultan abi: Don't just give a formula; show the beauty of the idea. If a student falls in love with the method, they will follow it to the end.

✨ Key Takeaways

  • Structured learning works: Progressive difficulty and logical sequencing significantly accelerate student progress in olympiad mathematics.
  • Expert guidance matters: Content created by experienced competitors resonates with students and builds confidence.
  • Immediate feedback is crucial: Students learn faster when they understand not just what's wrong, but why and how to improve.
  • Community potential: Students value both the learning platform and the connection to a community of serious mathematicians.
